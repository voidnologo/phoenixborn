# How to Play Well

You already know the rules — this page is about playing *better*. Ashes rewards patience, careful sequencing, and squeezing value from every die and card. Here are the ideas that separate a first game from a good one.

## The five things that decide most games

1. **Tempo — the activation dance.** Players alternate taking one action at a time, so *order matters enormously*. Don't dump your whole hand on your first activation; make the play that forces a response, then react to what the opponent does. The player who commits last to a combat usually wins it.
2. **Dice economy.** Ten dice is not a lot. Every die you spend on a card is a die you can't spend on your Phoenixborn's ability or a die power. Before spending, ask whether a **die power** (the free tool in the color's kit — 1 damage, −1 attack, a card, etc.) would do more this round.
3. **Card economy.** Hands are small and you rarely draw much, so every card is precious. Trade one card for two of theirs whenever you can, and lean on your deck's draw/recursion engine — running out of cards (fatigue) loses games on its own.
4. **The battlefield is small.** With only 4–6 unit slots for most Phoenixborn, board control is a knife-fight. A single well-timed removal spell or a good block can swing the whole game. Think about who profits from each trade before you attack.
5. **Use your Phoenixborn every round.** The ability is free-or-cheap value you get *every single round* — Water Blast, Spirit Burn, Hunter's Mark, Command Strike, and the rest. A round where you didn't use it is usually a round you fell behind.

## Attacking, blocking, and guarding

- **Attack when the math is in your favor**, not just because you can. A unit that attacks is exhausted and can't block next turn — count what you're exposing.
- **Guarding** lets a unit soak a hit meant for another target (or your Phoenixborn). Cheap guard bodies (Anchornaut, Biter, Shadow Guard) buy you time; keep one back when facing aggression.
- **Blocking** is often better than removal — it costs no dice. Save your removal spells for threats you *can't* profitably block.
- Watch for **Bypass** (can't be blocked or guarded) and **unit reactions**; the best players bait these out before committing.

## First Five and mulligan thinking

Your opening five cards are the most important decision you make each game. A good opening:

- **Does something on turn one** — usually one or two Summon spells so you have a board and a working spellboard by round's end.
- **Can be paid for** by your dice on the very first roll.
- **Includes a way to interact** — a removal or reaction spell so a fast opponent can't run you over.

If your hand can't act on turn one, or is all expensive/situational cards, that's a mulligan. It's better to reset than to spend a round doing nothing. (More on building a First Five in the [Deckbuilding Guide](deckbuilding.md).)

## Know the archetypes — and how to fight them

| Archetype | What it wants to do | How to beat it |
|---|---|---|
| **Aggro / Burn** (Brennen, Hope) | End the game in 2–3 rounds with direct damage | Stabilize the board early, gain life/tempo, trade efficiently — survive the opening rush and you win the long game |
| **Midrange / Beatdown** (Rin, Odette) | Deploy bigger, better units and attack | Answer their key threat with removal, then win the grind or race under them |
| **Control** (Echo, Jessa, Harold) | Trade 1-for-1 until they win the long game | Apply steady pressure, force them to answer everything, don't overcommit into a board wipe |
| **Combo** (Maeoni, James) | Assemble a specific engine (e.g. a giant Silver Snake) | Disrupt the key piece, pressure their life so they can't durdle, hold removal for the payoff |
| **Swarm / Tokens** (Koji, Namine) | Flood the board and go wide | Board wipes and area effects (One Hundred Blades, Meteor, Nature's Wrath) are gold; don't trade 1-for-1 |
| **Mill** (Harold's Angry Druid) | Empty your draw deck so you deck out | Close the game fast, or play efficiently so you're not the one running low |

## A few habits that level you up fast

- **Plan the whole round, then act.** Look at your dice, your hand, and the board before your first activation.
- **Hold reactions.** A Golden Veil or Particle Shield saved for the right moment is worth three played early.
- **Count lethal every turn** — both yours and theirs. Knowing the opponent's maximum damage tells you how much you can safely tap out.
- **Don't fear chump-blocking.** Trading a 1-life token to save 3 life on your Phoenixborn is usually correct against aggro.
- **Re-pilot a deck several times.** Ashes decks reward knowing your lines; the third game with a deck is a different experience from the first.
