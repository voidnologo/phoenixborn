# References & Further Reading

The decks and theory on this site draw on the following sources. The four articles by Jayson Seth Lindley are the most directly useful — he both catalogs the strongest tournament decks and teaches the deckbuilding fundamentals.

## Deckbuilding theory & multi-deck collections

- Jayson Seth Lindley — *Colorful Cubes: Foundations of Deckbuilding in Ashes Reborn* — <https://jaysonsethlindley.medium.com/colorful-cubes-foundations-of-deckbuilding-in-ashes-reborn-c49cd1abbe29>
- Jayson Seth Lindley — *Adventuring Party 4: Seven Constructed Ashes Decks From One Collection* — <https://jaysonsethlindley.medium.com/adventuring-party-4-seven-constructed-ashes-decks-from-one-collection-75842c7d4a3a>
- Jayson Seth Lindley — *First Adventure: Dueling Decks from the Ashes Reborn Master Set* — <https://jaysonsethlindley.medium.com/first-adventure-dueling-decks-from-the-ashes-reborn-master-set-1d82917881fb>

- **ashteki.com** — the community online-play client and deck browser — <https://ashteki.com/>

- Ashes Reborn Wiki — *Card Errata* — <https://wiki.ashes.live/books/rulebook/page/card-errata>

!!! note
    All decklists were validated against the ashes.live card database, and all card counts against the box-contents model. A handful of cards were adjusted from their original tournament lists to respect the current (Ascendancy-era) errata and to keep all seven decks simultaneously buildable from one collection; those swaps are noted with each deck.
